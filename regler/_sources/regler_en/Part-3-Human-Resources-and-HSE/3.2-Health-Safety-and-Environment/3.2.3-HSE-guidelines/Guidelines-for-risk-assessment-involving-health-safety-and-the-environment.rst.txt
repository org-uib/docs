Guidelines for risk assessment involving health, safety and the environment.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

:doc:`Norwegian version </regler/Del-3-Personal-og-HMS/3.2-Helse-miljoe-og-sikkerhet/3.2.3-HMS-retningslinjer/Retningslinjer-for-risikovurdering-knyttet-til-helse-miljoe-og-sikkerhet>`

Approved by the University Director 18.12.2015, last modified nomvember 11, 2019, archive reference 2015/13765.

1. Purpose
~~~~~~~~~~

The guidelines must ensure that risk assessment for health and safety
and the environment at the University of Bergen (UiB) is carried out and
documented in line with the requirements of the authorities and internal
policies.

2. Legal basis
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The guidelines are included in UiB’s collection of rules (Part 3. Human
Resources and HSE) and are based on HSE legislation (see reference).

3. Scope
~~~~~~~~

The guidelines apply to work/activities under the auspices of UiB. If a
coordination agreement exists regarding health and safety and the
environment with any other establishment the applicable coordination
agreement must be followed.

4. Responsibilities
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Line managers are responsible for ensuring that the guidelines are
publicised and monitored within their area of responsibility.

5. Definitions
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

*Undesired incident:* Incident or situation that can cause harm
to/problems for humans, material goods or the environment.

*Risk assessment:* Identification of undesirable incidents, followed by
a systematic review of what might cause undesirable incidents and what
consequences these may have for the working and learning environment.
The risk assessment must identify risk-reduction measures and provide a
basis for the emergency preparedness work.

*Risk acceptance criteria:* `Predefined
criteria <http://ekstern.filer.uib.no/poa/Hms/Risikovurdering/RisikoakseptkriterierJuni.15.ENG.pdf>`__
forming the basis of any decision regarding acceptable risk.

*CIM:* Digital support tool for risk assessment and emergency
preparedness.

*Line manager*: The University Director, the Dean, faculty director,
department director and head of department or equivalent, who has the
ultimate responsibility for health and safety and the working
environment in the unit.

6. Organisation and approvals
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

**Within his/her area of responsibility, the line manager or his/her
representative shall:**

-  Appoint one or more resource persons to assist in the work of risk
   assessment.
-  Ensure the risk assessment is documented.
-  Ensure the risk assessment is carried out before activity starts and
   that it is updated regularly or in the event of changes that may
   affect the risks.
-  Take part in and carry out risk assessments consisting of:

*1) Planning*

-  Identification and description of areas for risk assessment.
-  Participation of user representatives (employees, students and safety
   delegates)

*2) Risk analysis*

-  Identification of undesired incidents that can occur within chosen
   areas.
-  Reviewing the causes and consequences of the undesired incidents.

*3) Evaluation and handling*

-  Comparison of findings with risk acceptance criteria.
-  Evaluation of existing measures.
-  Identify new measures to reduce probability and consequences.
-  The measures must be prioritised, form part of the Unit’s HSE action
   plan and be carried out.

**Resource person for risk assessment:**

-  Must be a driving force and supervisor for risk assessments.

**User representative:**

-  Must take part in the risk assessments and the development and
   carrying out of measures.

**The HSE Section:**

-  Can where necessary assist in risk assessment.
